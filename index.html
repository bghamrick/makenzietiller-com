Questions

Good things to know

Do I pay you anything?

No. My planning service is free to you. I'm paid a commission by the travel suppliers, and that commission is already built into the standard price of your trip.

Does using you cost more than booking myself?

No — you pay the same price you'd pay booking directly, and often less when I can apply current promotions. You never pay more for working with me.

How do you get paid?

The parks, resorts, and cruise lines pay advisors a commission out of the trip cost you'd be paying them anyway. It doesn't change your price — it just means you get a planner included.

What if something goes wrong during my trip?

Text me first and I'll help sort it out — usually within the hour. For true emergencies (medical, theft, lost passport), call local emergency services first, then loop me in.

Which destinations do you plan?

My focus is Universal Studios (Orlando and Hollywood), Disney (Walt Disney World and Disneyland), and Royal Caribbean cruises. I'm happy to plan beyond them too — just ask.

How far in advance should I book?

The earlier the better — the best dining, excursions, and room categories go first, especially on holiday dates. As soon as you have a rough window, reach out and I'll tell you exactly when to lock things in.

I'm not sure where to go yet — can you still help?

Absolutely. Bring a vibe, a budget, or just "we need a break" and I'll help you figure out the right trip. Start anywhere and I'll take it from there.
