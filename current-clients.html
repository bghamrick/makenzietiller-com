Disney Days FAQ

When should I book MagicBands or MagicBand+?

Order them at least 3–4 weeks before your trip so they ship to your home. If you wait, you can still pick them up at the resort, but you'll miss the convenience of having them set up in advance. I'll send a reminder and walk you through linking park tickets, dining, and Lightning Lane purchases.

How does Disney's Lightning Lane / Genie+ work right now?

Disney's paid line-skip product gets renamed and re-priced regularly, so the specifics shift. Day-of, you'll book individual ride times through the My Disney Experience app, usually starting at 7 a.m. I'll send you a current strategy doc tailored to your park days and group — including which rides are worth paying extra for and which ones really aren't.

Do I need park reservations on top of my ticket?

It depends on your ticket type and the dates of your trip — Disney's park-reservation requirement has been loosening but isn't fully gone in all scenarios. I'll confirm what your specific booking needs and lock the reservations in for you when the window opens.

Cruise Nights FAQ

When do I need a passport vs. a birth certificate?

A passport is always the safest option and is required for any cruise that starts or ends in a foreign port. For most closed-loop cruises out of U.S. ports (more on those below), U.S. citizens can sail with a state-issued birth certificate plus a government-issued photo ID. If you need to fly home from a foreign port in an emergency, you'll need a passport — so I recommend one for every cruiser whenever possible.

What's a closed-loop cruise?

A closed-loop cruise begins and ends at the same U.S. port — e.g., Miami to the Bahamas and back to Miami. These have looser documentation rules for U.S. citizens. An open-loop cruise starts and ends in different ports (or in a foreign port) and requires a valid passport for every traveler, no exceptions.

When should I book shore excursions, dining, and drink packages?

As early as the cruise line opens pre-cruise planning — usually 60–120 days before sailing depending on the line and your status. Popular excursions and specialty dining sell out fast, especially on Disney Cruise Line and on holiday sailings. I'll send a planning checklist with each window so you don't miss the good stuff.
